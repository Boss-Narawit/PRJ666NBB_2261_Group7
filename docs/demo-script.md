# ReDrobe Live Demo Script

Tap-by-tap script for the live-app portion of the 15-minute pitch. Pairs with the
Figma deck (13 slides) — timestamps assume ~2.5 min of talking (slides 1–4) before
the demo starts, and ~2.5 min of talking (slides 10–13) after it ends.

Demo account: **demo@redrobe.app / Demo2026pitch**

---

## Setup (do this before the room fills up)

1. Backend: `cd backend && npm run dev` (port 3000)
2. Metro: `cd frontend && npx react-native start --reset-cache`
3. App: `cd frontend && npx react-native run-ios --simulator "iPhone 17 Pro"`
4. AI service on port 8000, then `cd backend && node scripts/backfillEmbeddings.js`
5. **Load a demo photo into the simulator's Photos app** — the live AI similarity
   check picks from the photo library, not a camera. Drag a camel/beige coat photo
   onto the running simulator window (it lands in Photos automatically). Do this
   _before_ opening the app, not mid-demo.
6. Fresh data: `node scripts/seedDemoAccount.js`, then verify: `node scripts/verifyDemoAccount.js` (expect 21 PASS)
7. Log in as the demo account. Leave the app open on the **Dashboard** (home tab).
8. Know your tab bar — icons only, no text labels, left to right:
   **home** (Dashboard/Wardrobe/Wear History/Export) · **compass** (new AI similarity
   check) · **cart** (your pending purchases) · **bell** (notifications) · **person** (profile).

---

## 1. Dashboard tour (~1 min)

_(bridges from slide 4 — "one loop, four moves")_

- You're on the Dashboard. Point at the stat cards: **Total Items**, forgotten count,
  and the **Wardrobe Utilization** bar (90-day window, BR24).
- Scroll to **My Wardrobe** → note the forgotten-item preview cards each have an
  inline **"Wear"** button — logging a wear from the dashboard is one tap, no
  navigation required.
- Say: "Everything below comes from the same source of truth — outfit logs. Let's
  look at where it starts."

## 2. Digital Wardrobe (~1.5 min)

_(slide 5 — "Every item, one place")_

1. Tap **"See All"** next to My Wardrobe → lands on **My Wardrobe** screen.
2. Point out the grid — 11 active items. Archived and exported items are _not_ here
   by design (positive status filter — keeps the working closet clean).
3. Tap **White Oxford Shirt** (or any item) → opens Item Detail.
4. Edit a field inline (e.g. tap the size row, change it, confirm) — show it persists
   after backing out and back in.
5. Back out. Tap the **"⋮"** on an item card in the grid → Alert menu:
   **"View Details" / "Archive"**. Don't archive live (would disturb seeded data) —
   just show the menu exists, then Cancel.

## 3. Wear Analytics + Forgotten Items (~1.5 min)

_(slide 6 — "Know what you actually wear")_

1. From Item Detail or Wardrobe, go back to the home tab root, then navigate to
   **Wear History**.
2. Scroll to **today** — point out **two separate named outfits logged the same
   day**: "Campus Day" and "Dinner Out." Say: "BR8 — as many outfits per day as
   real life needs, not one slot."
3. Tap into one outfit → **Wear Log Detail** → back out.
4. Navigate to **Forgotten Items** (from Dashboard or wardrobe overflow). Point out
   **Silk Scarf** (40 days) and **Corduroy Pants** (35 days) against the 14-day
   threshold — say these counts are 100% derived from the logs you just saw, never
   hand-set (BR9).
5. Tap the **bell icon** (Notifications tab) → show the two unread forgotten-item
   alerts sitting there, generated by the same threshold.

## 4. Thoughtful Purchasing — the headline (~3 min)

_(slides 7 + 8 — 24h cooldown, then the AI similarity check — give this the most time)_

**4a. The cooldown timer (cart icon)**

1. Tap the **cart icon** (bottom tab) → your pending purchases.
2. **Camel Trench Coat** — status `pending`, cooldown shows **"~22h left"**, the
   confirm button reads **"Locked"** and is disabled. Say: "BR15 — the button is
   physically un-tappable until the timer runs out. This isn't a UI suggestion,
   it's enforced."
3. **Cashmere Scarf** — cooldown already reads **"Cooling-off complete"**, button
   is now **"Buy it"** (enabled). Tap it live:
   - It confirms the purchase, then **navigates you into "Add Item"** prefilled
     with the name and photo — the loop closes back into the wardrobe catalog you
     showed in step 2. Narrate this, then back out **without saving** (avoid
     polluting the wardrobe grid mid-demo) — tap the back chevron.
4. **White Canvas Sneakers** — already `approved`, just point at the status badge.

**4b. The live AI similarity check (compass icon)**

1. Tap the **compass icon** (bottom tab) → new similarity check form.
2. Tap **"Tap to upload image"** → pick the camel/beige coat photo you pre-loaded
   into the simulator.
3. Type an item name, e.g. "Camel Trench Coat."
4. Tap **"AI Similarity Check"** — this is a **real, live call**: photo → fashion-clip
   embedding → Atlas `$vectorSearch` across your whole seeded wardrobe.
5. Modal pops up: **"AI Similarity Check"** with a percentage, a warning icon if
   ≥70%, and the matched item's thumbnail + name (expect it to surface **Camel
   Wool Coat**, already in the seeded wardrobe). Read the line out loud: _"This
   item is N% similar to 'Camel Wool Coat' in your wardrobe. Do you really need it?"_
6. Tap **"Cancel"** to close without creating a duplicate purchase (or tap
   **"Start Timer"** if you want to show the full create-to-cart flow — it'll land
   you back on the cart tab with a fresh pending entry).

> **Fallback:** if the AI service is down or the call errors, don't panic — open
> Notifications instead and point at the stored `similarity_alert` ("87% similar to
> your Camel Wool Coat"), and say "here's what that same check produced yesterday."
> Never present the error screen as if it were expected.

## 5. Circular Economy (~2 min)

_(slide 9 — "Nothing ends in the bin")_

**5a. The resale guardrail (BR21)**

1. Home tab → Wardrobe → tap **Ripped Band Tee** (condition: Damaged).
2. Tap **"Export/Donate"** → **Export Items** screen, **Resale** tab selected by
   default. Pick any active partner (e.g. ThredUp) → **Continue**.
3. **Quality Checklist** screen: tap through all 5 checks (tears, stains, buttons/
   zippers, photos, description) + the consent toggle → **"Confirm & Export."**
4. It fails server-side: the alert reads **"0 exported, 1 failed: Damaged items
   cannot be exported to resale partners."** Say: "This isn't a client-side
   nicety — the same rule is enforced in the service layer, so there's no way
   around it from the app."
5. Tap OK (pops back to Dashboard).

**5b. Completed exports (already in history)**

1. Person icon (Profile tab) → **"Export History"** row.
2. Show **Beige Trench Coat → ThredUp (resale)** and **Striped Linen Shirt →
   Goodwill (donation)** already completed.
3. Tap one → opens the item's read-only detail view with an **"Exported →
   {partner} · {date}"** banner — editing/export buttons are gone; the item is
   locked because its status is no longer `Available`.

## Wrap

Return to the Dashboard before switching back to slides (slide 10 onward is
talking-only — architecture, status, roadmap, thank you).

## Fallbacks

- **AI service down:** skip the live check in step 4b — use the stored
  `similarity_alert` in Notifications instead. Never fake a score.
- **Backend/Metro crashes mid-demo:** switch to pre-captured screenshots of the
  five flows above (capture these during the final rehearsal, not the night before).
- **State drifted from a rehearsal** (confirmed the Cashmere Scarf, archived an
  item, etc.): `node scripts/seedDemoAccount.js` rebuilds the demo account from
  scratch in ~10 seconds. Run it, then re-log-in.
- **Simulator has no demo photo loaded:** drag any coat/jacket photo onto the
  simulator window from Finder — it lands in Photos immediately, no rebuild needed.
